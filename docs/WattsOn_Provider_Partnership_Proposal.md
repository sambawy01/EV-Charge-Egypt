## 1. Executive Partnership Summary

### What WattsOn Offers Providers

WattsOn is a live, publicly accessible platform aggregating 435+ EV charging stations from 12 providers across Egypt. The platform runs on AI (Claude-powered copilot), supports 140+ EV models, and includes trip planning, vehicle-specific charging recommendations, and community-driven real-time station status reporting. It is not a concept deck. It is live at wattson-ev.vercel.app.

We offer providers three things they cannot build alone:

1. **Demand generation.** Every WattsOn user is an EV driver actively looking for a charger. We route them to partner stations through navigation, AI recommendations, and trip planning. This is not advertising. This is qualified, high-intent traffic delivered to your charger.

2. **Market intelligence.** We collect data on charging behavior, route patterns, station utilization, and user preferences across the entire Egyptian EV ecosystem. Partners receive analytics that no single-network app can produce -- because we see the whole market, not just one network.

3. **Brand amplification.** Our upcoming marketing campaign -- 20+ influencers, celebrity partnerships, PR coverage in major Egyptian outlets, and pre-installation on dealer tablets at BYD, MG, and Hyundai showrooms -- puts partner brands in front of every new EV buyer entering the Egyptian market.

### What We Want from Providers

- Verification of station data (locations, connector types, power levels, operating hours)
- For Tier 3+ partners: real-time API access to charger availability status
- For Tier 4 partners: payment processing integration and co-development commitment
- Permission to use provider branding on station cards within the app
- A point of contact for operational updates (new stations, maintenance, pricing changes)

### Why This Is a Win-Win

The Egyptian EV market is at an inflection point. Registrations are accelerating, new models from BYD, MG, and Hyundai are flooding showrooms, and the government's EV infrastructure push is creating new charging providers monthly. The problem is fragmentation. Drivers do not know where to charge. Stations sit underutilized. Providers spend money on customer acquisition that never converts.

WattsOn eliminates fragmentation. We become the single interface between every EV driver in Egypt and every charging station. Providers who partner early get preferred positioning in the platform that every driver will use. Providers who wait get listed anyway -- but without branding, without priority, and without the data.

**The math is simple:** if WattsOn delivers even 50 additional charging sessions per month to a partner's network at an average of 150 EGP per session, that is 7,500 EGP in incremental revenue -- against a Tier 2 cost of 500 EGP/month. That is a 15x return before counting brand value, data insights, or reduced churn.

## 2. The Pitch -- Why Providers Should Partner

### The Problems Every Charging Provider in Egypt Faces

**Low station utilization.** Industry estimates suggest average utilization across Egyptian charging networks runs between 15-25%. That means 75-85% of the time, your chargers are idle -- generating zero revenue while costing you rent, electricity standby, and maintenance. Every empty charger is a stranded asset.

**No scalable channel to reach EV drivers.** You can post on Instagram. You can put a sign on the highway. But there is no systematic way to reach an EV driver at the exact moment they need a charge and route them to your station. Your own app only reaches your existing customers. The driver who has never heard of you -- the one buying a BYD Dolphin next month -- has no way to find your network.

**Competitors are poaching your customers.** When a driver pulls up to your station and finds it occupied or out of service, they open Google Maps and drive to the nearest alternative. That alternative might be a competitor. You just lost a customer -- not because your product is worse, but because you had no way to communicate availability in real time.

**No real-time feedback loop.** When a connector is broken, a payment terminal is down, or your parking spots are blocked by ICE vehicles, how long does it take you to find out? Hours? Days? Your customers discover these problems, get frustrated, and leave a bad Google review. You find out when someone screenshots it and sends it to the ops team.

**High customer acquisition cost.** Digital advertising for EV charging is expensive because the audience is small and the targeting is imprecise. Facebook does not have an "owns an EV and needs to charge right now" targeting option. Every new customer you acquire through traditional marketing costs multiples of what a single charging session generates.

**Invisible to new EV buyers.** When someone buys their first EV in Egypt, their immediate question is: "Where do I charge?" They ask the dealer, they ask Facebook groups, they ask friends. If your network is not in the answer to that question, you do not exist for that buyer. And once they establish a charging routine with a competitor, switching costs are high.

### What WattsOn Solves

**We drive traffic to your stations.** WattsOn's AI copilot, trip planner, and proximity search all route users to charging stations. For partner networks, this routing is prioritized. When a BYD Atto 3 driver in New Cairo asks "Where should I charge?", the AI recommends partner stations first -- factoring in connector compatibility (CCS, Type 2), charging speed, distance, and real-time availability.

**We give you a direct channel to every EV driver in Egypt.** Not just your customers. All of them. Every WattsOn user sees partner stations with verified badges, branded cards, and priority placement. This is not a banner ad they scroll past. This is the interface they use every time they need to charge.

**Real-time status data reduces wasted trips.** When users report station conditions -- available, occupied, out of service, ICE blocked -- every subsequent user benefits. For API-integrated partners, this data flows automatically. Fewer wasted trips means happier customers means higher retention.

**Community ratings build your reputation.** WattsOn's community review system lets users rate station experiences. High-rated stations surface higher in recommendations. This creates a virtuous cycle: good stations get more traffic, which generates more positive reviews, which drives even more traffic.

### "But We Already Show Availability in Our Own App"

Yes — and that's exactly why you should integrate with WattsOn. Here's the reality:

**Your app only reaches YOUR users.** The Infinity EV app has great real-time availability. But it only serves people who already downloaded the Infinity app, already have an Infinity account, and already know Infinity exists. That's maybe 20% of EV drivers in Egypt. The other 80% — the BYD driver who just bought their first EV, the tourist renting an EV in Hurghada, the fleet manager evaluating providers — they will never download 12 different provider apps.

**Drivers don't want 12 apps.** Nobody installs Elsewedy Plug AND Sha7en AND IKARUS AND Infinity AND Electra AND KarmCharge apps. They want ONE app that shows EVERYTHING. That's WattsOn. When you share your availability data with us, you don't lose anything — your own app still works. But now your availability data reaches 5-10x more drivers than before.

**Availability data DRIVES TRAFFIC TO YOU.** When a driver sees "Infinity EV - CPC Sharm: 3/4 Available ✅" on WattsOn and "Competitor Station: No data ❓" next to it, which one do they drive to? The one with confirmed availability. Sharing your data isn't giving away an advantage — it IS the advantage. The provider with real-time data on WattsOn wins the customer every time.

**Without integration, you get crowdsourced data instead.** We already show station status through community reports. But crowdsourced data is less accurate and less timely than your real-time feed. If you don't integrate, drivers see "📡 Community reported: Available · 2h ago" instead of "✅ Live: 3/4 Available (real-time)". Which builds more trust in your brand?

**The aggregator always wins.** Google Maps aggregated restaurant data. Booking.com aggregated hotels. Skyscanner aggregated flights. The providers who integrated early got the most traffic. The ones who refused became invisible. EV charging will follow the same pattern — the question isn't whether an aggregator will dominate, it's whether you'll be a featured partner or a generic pin on the map.

**What we need from you is simple:**
- An API endpoint that returns: station_id, connector_id, status (available/occupied/offline), last_updated
- We poll every 2-5 minutes (or you push via webhook)
- No sensitive data — just charger status
- We display it as "✅ Live Status" with your branding and logo

**What you get in return:**
- Your availability data reaches every EV driver in Egypt, not just your app users
- "Live Status" badge that competitors without integration don't get
- AI preferentially recommends stations with confirmed availability
- Fewer wasted trips to your stations = higher customer satisfaction
- Data analytics on how users interact with your stations on WattsOn

### WattsOn as Your Payment Gateway

The real unlock isn't just showing availability — it's letting drivers **pay through WattsOn**. Here's why this transforms the business for both sides:

**The Problem for Drivers Today:**
- Each provider requires their own app, their own account, their own payment method
- A driver traveling Cairo → Hurghada might need 3 different apps to pay at 3 different stations
- Some stations only accept specific payment methods or prepaid credits
- New EV buyers are overwhelmed: "I just want to charge and pay, why do I need 12 accounts?"

**The Problem for Providers:**
- Payment processing is expensive to build and maintain (PCI compliance, fraud, refunds)
- Onboarding new customers is friction-heavy (download app → create account → add payment → verify)
- Payment failures = lost revenue (card declines, expired sessions, app crashes)
- Cash collection at unmanned stations is impossible
- Each provider reinvents the same payment wheel

**WattsOn as the Universal Charging Payment:**

One account. One wallet. Every station in Egypt.

The driver tops up their WattsOn wallet once (credit card, Fawry, Vodafone Cash, InstaPay) and can pay at ANY partner station — Infinity, Elsewedy, Sha7en, IKARUS, Electra — without downloading anything else.

**How it works:**

1. **Driver arrives at station** → WattsOn detects proximity
2. **Selects charger** → sees connector type, power, and price per kWh
3. **Taps "Start Charging"** → WattsOn initiates the session via provider's API
4. **Charges their car** → real-time kWh counter in the app
5. **Session ends** → WattsOn deducts from wallet, settles with provider

**Revenue Model:**

| Party | Per Session |
|---|---|
| Provider receives | 93% of charging fee |
| WattsOn commission | 5% |
| Payment processor | 2% |

**Example:** A 30 kWh charge at 3.5 EGP/kWh = 105 EGP total
- Provider gets: 97.65 EGP
- WattsOn gets: 5.25 EGP
- Payment processor: 2.10 EGP

**Why providers should want this:**

1. **Zero payment infrastructure cost.** WattsOn handles PCI compliance, fraud detection, refunds, chargebacks, and multi-method payment processing. The provider focuses on hardware and electricity — we handle the money.

2. **Instant customer base.** Every WattsOn user can pay at your station on day one. No app download, no account creation, no payment setup. The friction that costs you 70% of potential customers at the onboarding step? Gone.

3. **Higher utilization = higher revenue.** More payment options = more completed sessions. The driver who would have driven past because they didn't have your app? Now they stop and charge. At 5% commission on incremental revenue that wouldn't have existed otherwise, it's pure profit for both sides.

4. **Multiple payment methods you don't support.** WattsOn accepts:
   - Credit/debit cards (Visa, Mastercard)
   - Fawry (Egypt's largest bill payment network — 200K+ points of sale)
   - Vodafone Cash / Orange Money / Etisalat Cash (mobile wallets)
   - InstaPay (instant bank transfers)
   - Apple Pay / Google Pay
   - WattsOn prepaid wallet (top-up model)

   Most providers only support credit cards. WattsOn opens charging to the 60% of Egyptians who primarily use mobile money or cash-based payment.

5. **Automated settlement.** Provider receives daily/weekly settlement reports with full breakdown: which station, which connector, kWh delivered, amount earned, commission deducted. Direct bank transfer, no invoicing hassle.

6. **Fleet billing.** Fleet managers can set up company accounts with centralized billing. All their drivers charge on one account, one invoice. This is a feature most providers can't offer alone.

**For the Strategic Alliance tier:**

Partners who integrate payment through WattsOn get:
- 5% commission (vs 15-30% on other platforms) — we take less because we want adoption
- First 6 months at 3% commission for founding partners
- Real-time settlement dashboard
- Dispute resolution handled by WattsOn
- Branded receipt: "Charged at [Provider Name] via WattsOn"
- Tax-compliant invoicing for B2B/fleet customers

**The bigger picture:**

WattsOn becomes the **Visa of EV charging** — the payment layer that sits between every driver and every station. Providers don't compete on payment UX anymore; they compete on station quality, location, and price. Everyone wins:

- **Drivers:** One wallet, every station, no friction
- **Providers:** More customers, zero payment headaches, daily settlement
- **WattsOn:** 5% of every kWh charged in Egypt flows through the platform

**This is how we become indispensable.** Once drivers load money into WattsOn and providers receive payments through WattsOn, switching costs are high for everyone. The aggregator isn't just a map anymore — it's the financial infrastructure of Egypt's EV charging ecosystem.

**Our marketing campaign markets you.** When our influencer content goes viral showing a celebrity charging at an Infinity EV station, that is free brand exposure for Infinity. Every WattsOn marketing touchpoint that shows a map of stations is marketing your network. You do not pay extra for it.

Which of your locations has the most navigation requests but the lowest utilization (signaling an accessibility or experience problem)? What car models are your most frequent users driving, and are you offering the right connector mix? We can answer all of this.

## 5. Data Value Proposition

WattsOn sits at the intersection of supply (charging stations) and demand (EV drivers). This position generates data that no single provider can access alone. Partners receive data calibrated to their tier level.

### Station Utilization Heatmaps

Visual maps showing which of your stations get the most views, navigation requests, and community check-ins. Identify your highest-performing locations and your underperformers. Compare weekday vs. weekend patterns. Spot seasonal trends (Red Sea stations surge in summer; compound stations peak during Ramadan evenings).

### Peak Charging Hours by Location

Hour-by-hour demand curves for each station in your network. Know when your stations are busiest and when they are empty. Use this data to optimize staffing, maintenance scheduling, and dynamic pricing. Example insight: "Your Madinaty station sees 73% of its traffic between 6-9 PM. Your New Cairo Downtown station peaks at 11 AM-1 PM. Consider time-of-use pricing to spread demand."

### User Demographics

Aggregated, anonymized data on the drivers navigating to your stations:
- **Vehicle mix:** Which EV models are your most frequent users driving? Are you serving mostly BYD Atto 3 owners (60kW max charge) or BMW iX owners (195kW max)? This informs your connector and power level investment decisions.
- **Geographic distribution:** Where do your users live vs. where do they charge? Are you attracting local residents or highway travelers?
- **Charging frequency:** How often do your users charge per week? Are they daily commuters or weekend road-trippers?

### Trip Route Patterns

Anonymized, aggregated data on where WattsOn users are driving. Which corridors have the highest EV traffic? Where are the charging deserts -- high-traffic routes with no stations? This data is gold for network expansion planning. Example: "412 trip plans in the last 30 days included the Ain Sokhna-Hurghada corridor. Only 3 stations exist on this 300km stretch. Users report range anxiety as the #1 concern."

### Competitive Benchmarking

How your stations rank against the market on key metrics:
- Navigation request share (% of total navigations going to your network vs. competitors)
- Community rating comparison (your average rating vs. market average)
- Connector coverage gap analysis (are competitors offering CCS2 in locations where you only have Type 2?)
- Geographic coverage overlap (where are you competing head-to-head, and where do you have exclusive territory?)

### Community Sentiment Analysis

What users are saying about your stations in reviews and community reports:
- Common praise themes (fast charging, good location, clean facility)
- Common complaint themes (payment issues, connector damage, no shade)
- Sentiment trend over time (improving or declining?)
- Specific actionable feedback (e.g., "14 users at your Zayed station reported the CCS connector on charger #2 is intermittent")

### Predictive Demand Forecasting

Using historical data, weather patterns, event calendars, and EV registration trends to forecast:
- Expected demand by station for the next 30/60/90 days
- Optimal locations for new station deployment
- Revenue projections based on traffic trends

All 12 providers currently on WattsOn are listed at Tier 1 (Basic Aggregation). Their stations appear as generic pins on the map. No branding. No priority. No data.

Every other provider's generic pins look worse by comparison. Drivers learn to trust the verified badge. They learn that Infinity stations show real-time availability and estimated charge times. They develop a preference.

**The competitive dynamics are straightforward:**

1. The first provider to reach Tier 3 in a region gets the "Recommended" badge for that region. This is an exclusive benefit. If Infinity is the Recommended partner for the Cairo-Alexandria corridor, no other provider gets that badge on that corridor.

2. AI recommendations favor partners. When a user asks "Where should I charge?", the AI weighs partnership tier in its ranking. Two stations equidistant from the user -- one verified, one not -- the verified station wins. Every time.

3. Trip planner defaults to partners. A Cairo-to-Hurghada route with three Sha7en stations and two unverified stations along the way? The trip planner recommends Sha7en. The unverified stations appear as alternatives buried in the details.

**This creates a network effect that compounds over time.** Partner stations get more traffic, more reviews, higher ratings, better data -- which makes them rank even higher -- which drives even more traffic. Non-partners fall further behind each month.

## 8. The Ask -- Specific Next Steps

### Meeting Structure

Every provider engagement follows the same disciplined 5-step process. We do not waste time with vague "let's explore synergies" conversations. Every meeting has a clear objective and a clear next step.

**Step 5: Partnership Signing and Launch (Week 4-6)**

- Contract signing with agreed tier and terms
